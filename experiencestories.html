Vasu


My journey at KP started in late 2015 when Prashanth B began pitching the idea to me and others on our drives from Thiruvanmiyur to the Good Life Centre, Chennai where we used to volunteer. The idea of using one's skills and education to give to the community is always enticing. When asked if I would be on board I agreed. Being a mechanical engineer with a knack for building things I believed there were avenues where I could contribute to the organization. I was assigned to Project Hiko Noka (then Drona) where we wanted to build a drone for use in agriculture. I was initially responsible for the mechanical design but as with most projects with small teams one gets involved in every aspect of the project. Over a year later and 13,700 km away I'm now looking into the code that runs the motors of the drone. There were times of fast and slow progress but progress nonetheless. With new additions in the team and new outlooks, we seem to be gathering pace on this project. With the emphasis on skills and working in teams, one learns how to get things done, which can be useful in many areas. I'm personally looking to take full advantage of this and look forward to the deployment of our creations.
